Timeline

  • National Housing Act June 1934
    The Federal Housing Administration (FHA) is created to improve housing conditions by insuring mortgages.
  • Housing Act of 1937 Sep 1937
    The US Housing Authority is created to subsidize local public housing agencies to improve housing conditions for low-income families.
  • GI Bill Jun 1944
    The GI Bill is passed, providing low interest and guaranteed mortgages to WW2 veterans.
  • Dep. Housing and Urban Development Sep 1965
    The HUD is created to move various housing programs to the presidential cabinet's oversight. Its goal is to increase homeownership, assist development, and create more affordable, discrimination-free housing opportunities.
  • Fair Housing Act Apr 1968
    The Fair Housing Act of 1968 is passed, prohibiting housing discrimination.
  • Subsidized Housing Moratorium Jan 1973
    Nixon and the HUD issue a moratorium on subsidized housing programs and call for strict new guidelines to urban renewal.
  • Section 8 Aug 1974
    The Housing and Community Development Act of 1974 creates Section 8 to assist the low income with housing opportunities and conditions.
  • Low Income Housing Tax Credit Oct 1986
    Reagan introduces Low Income Housing Tax Credit to encourage construction and rehabilitation of low-income housing.
