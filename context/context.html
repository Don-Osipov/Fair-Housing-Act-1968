Historical Context

[The family of J.D. Shelley pictured at their home, 4600 Labadie Ave. From left, Mrs. Minerva Coleman, a daughter, holding her son Jesse Jr.; Chatlee, Mrs. Shelley, Mr. Shelley, Leatha and J.D. Jr. - 1948]

Shelley v. Kraemer (1948)

Restrictive covenants were legal contracts prohibiting the sale of houses to a particular group of people, especially African Americans. Shelley v. Kraemer prohibited state enforcement of these covenants but still allowed private parties to abide by them if they wished. Thus, the effect of this decision was minimal, and restrictive covenants remained prevalent until outlawed by the Fair Housing Act of 1968.

[Scherman, David E. First African American troops the United. 1942]

World War II and the GI Bill

The GI Bill supported war veterans, with benefits like low interest and guaranteed mortgages and loans. However, black veterans usually did not qualify due to restrictive criteria, and had trouble finding houses. The Department of Veteran Affairs could not administer the promised low-interest mortgages itself, so white-run banks refused mortgages and loans to black people.

Civil Rights Act of 1964

Signed on July 2nd, 1964 by Johnson to prevent discrimination in public spaces, integrate schools, and make employment discrimination illegal.

It paved the way for the Fair Housing Act in 1968, which banned discrimination in the sale, rental, and financing of property.

The Assassination of Martin Luther King Jr.

On April 4th, the day of the Senate vote, Martin Luther King was assassinated in Tennessee. This provoked outrage, pressuring Congress to pass the new civil rights law. It passed on April 10th, and Johnson signed it the following day.

Levittown

At its creation, Levittown, PA was meant to be a white-only neighborhood. However, while its management refused to sell homes to non-Caucasians, private homeowners could. William and Daisy Myers, the first blacks in Levittown, obtained their house this way. They moved in to a mob throwing rocks and threatening them. The Myers received support from various groups and people within the community, however. Their battle against discrimination raised awareness for other people of color, and helped introduce the Fair Housing Act in 1966.

“Little Boxes” composed by Malvina Reynolds, performed by Pete Seeger in 1963

This song satirizes suburbia and conformist attitudes. Levittown was an example of a modest suburbia with families trying to be like each other, having the same ideal life. The convention of the time was a suburban house, a respectable job, and a family.
